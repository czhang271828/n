Filter

Definition

We call the non-empty subfamily F⊂P(X) a filter (滤子) whenever

  • A,B∈F implies A∩B∈F (downward closed),
  • A∈F implies {U∈P(X)∣A⊂U}⊂F (upward closed),

Cauchy filter

We call (G,+) an Abelian group whenever

  1. G is closed under the binary operation +, that is,

    ∀a,b∈G,a+b∈G.

  2. G​ admits associativity, that is,

    ∀a,b,c∈G,(a+b)+c=a+(b+c)=:a+b+c.

  3. G​ has a (additive) unit, that is,

    ∃e∈G s.t. ∀g∈G,g+e=e+g=g.

  4. ∀g∈G has an (additive) inverse, that is,

    ∀g∈G,∃g~∈G s.t. g+g~=g~+g=e.

  5. G​ (additively) commutes, that is,

    ∀a,b∈G,a+b=b+a.

Completeness

Definition

Consider the injection i:Q→R,r↦r. Then

  • Q→i(Q) is a homeomorphism.
  • i(Q) is dense in R.
  • R is complete in sense of Cauchy sequences, that is, each Cauchy sequence in i(Q) converges in R.

Completeness in general

The completeness of A∈Obj(TopAb) is a morphism f∈Mor(TopAb) such that

  • f:A→f(A) is a homeomorphism.
  • f(A) is dense in A~.
  • A~ is complete in sense of Cauchy filters (either in A or A~), that is, each Cauchy filter converges to exactly one point in A~.

Introduction of p-adic valuation

p-adic number

The standard absolute value |⋅|∞ (or |⋅| for simplicity) is induced by the standard topology for R (and its subspaces). We say x is close enough to y whence |x−y|∞≪1.

We shall define a new absolute value |⋅|p on Q, which is called p-adic absolute value, as follows.

The p-valuation of d∈Z is defined by the powers of p it contains, i.e.,

vp(d):=sup{n∈Z∣d⋅p−n∈Z}.

One may observe vp(d1d2)=vp(d1)+vp(d2), thus the domain of vp can be extended to Q.

Here vp(0)=vp(0)+vp(d) is well-defined since one can set ∞+k=∞.

Define |r|p:=p−vp(r) for r∈Q. Here |0|p=p−∞=0.

For each x∈Q​, there exists a unique k0∈Z and unique factorisation

x=∑k≥k0ak0pk0,ak∈{0,1,…,p−1}.

Thus vp(x)=−k0 and |x|p=pk0. Such factorisation is called the standard representation of p-adic numbers.

For instance, |9|3=3−2, |28|7=7−1.
