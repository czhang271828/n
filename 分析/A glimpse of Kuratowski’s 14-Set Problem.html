A glimpse of Kuratowski’s 14-Set Problem

The Original Problem

Problem. Let S∈P(Rn). Let k, I and c​ be unary operators such that

k(S)=S―,I(S)=S,c(S)=Rn−S.

Prove that there at most 14 sets generated by S.

Proof Sketch.

Step I The unary operator i(S)=S∘ can be generated by k and c, i.e., ckc=i.

Step II Let ⟨I,i,c,k⟩ be the free semigroup, which consists of i2, icik, k3i, etc.

Let M be the quotient semigroup ⟨I,i,c,k∣reduction of set operators⟩. For instance, Ik=I, c3=c, i2=i under such quotient relations. Then, {xS∣x∈M} consists of all possible sets generated by S.

Step III Utilise the relations ic=ck, kc=ci, c2=I, to move the word c to the initial of any y∈M. For instance, cikci=ki2.

Step IV Prove that i2=i, k2=k, ik=ikik, ki=kiki.

Step V Find that M={I,i,k,ik,ki,iki,kik,c,ci,ck,cik,cki,ciki,ckik}

Ex1 We define x≤y for each x,y∈M whenever x(S′)⊂y(S′) for each S∈Rn, where x<y whenever x(S′)⊊y(S′). Find the partially ordered structure of M.

Step VI Find an S such that M(S) consists of 14 distinct sets. For simplicity, one can just find the union of all pairwise disjoint examples which verifying the partially ordered structure.
