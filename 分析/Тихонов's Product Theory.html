Critical graphs are finite graphs

Definiton 1. We call G=G(V,E) a simple graph whenenver V is a set and E⊂{{x,y}∣x,y∈V,x≠y}. Here V (or E) is the set of vertices (or edges).

Simple graph is always unweighted, undirected, without self-loops and multi-edges.

Definition 2. Let G be a simple graph. G is k-colourable on vertices whenever there exists a function f∈{1,2,…,k}V s.t. f(x)≠f(y) when {x,y}∈E.

Definition 3. The minimal number for vertex colouring is the minimal positive integer kmin=:χ(G), such that G is kmin-colourable on vertices.

Definition 4. The vertex-deleted graphs of G are in the form of

Gx0(V′,E′):=Gx0({x∈V∣x≠x0},{{x,y}∈E∣x,y≠x0}).

Informally speaking, Gx0 is obtained from G by deleting x0 and all edges connecting to it.

Definition 5. We call a simple graph G critical whenever χ(G)<∞ and

supx∈Gχ(Gx)=maxx∈Gχ(Gx)<χ(G).

Main problem. Proves that all critical graphs are finite graphs, that is, |V|<∞ for each critical graph G(V,E).

The axiom of chioce is required when necessary.

The main problem is a straight corollary of the following marvelous theorem.

De Bruijn–Erdős theorem. Let G(V,E) be a infinite graph, that is, |V|≥|N|. If χ(F)≤k for each finite subgraph F in G, then χ(G)≤k.

Proof. Let S={1,2,…,k}, X=kV(G) be the product topology space (X,τ). Let F⊂τ denotes all possible schemes of colouring of every finite subgraphs in G. Then F consists of closed sets in (X,τ) and each finite subset in F has non-empty intersection. Since kV(G) is compact, ∩F is nonempty (by FICC.).
