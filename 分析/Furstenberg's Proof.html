Furstenberg's proof. Define the topology on Z, that is, open sets are generated by arithmetic sequences (in the form of aZ+b). We denote

S(a,b):=aZ+b:={an+b∣n∈Z}.

Then S(a,b) are both open and closed.

Assume that prime numbers are finite. Then ∪p is primeS(p,0) is a finite union of closed sets, thus closed. As a result, {±1}=Z−∪p is primeS(p,0) is open. This would be a contradiction!
